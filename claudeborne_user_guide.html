CAD 1901 · MCAR 2016 Part 7 (FTL) · Part 8 (Licensing) · ICAO Annex 1 — step-by-step for new users
ClaudeBorne is a web-based electronic pilot logbook. No app download needed — open it in any browser on any device. You sign in with your Google account.
  1. 1Open your browser and go to claudeborne.my
  2. 2Click Sign in with Google and select your registered Google account
  3. 3You'll land on the current month's logbook automatically
  4. 4First time? Click the ⚙ gear icon top-right and fill in your Profile — name, licence number, and airline — before entering any flights
  5. 5You're ready to log your first flight
Works on phones, tablets, and computers. No installation required.

⑦ ↑ 30 rows per page — click any cell to enter or edit data
Each row = one flight sector. Fill left to right — entering block times (STD/STA) is all you need for the app to calculate day and night hours automatically.
  1. 1Click any empty cell in the next blank row to activate it
  2. 2Date — type the day number (e.g. 15) or full date (15 MAY)
  3. 3Aircraft Type — e.g. B737-800
  4. 4Markings — aircraft registration, e.g. 9M-LDG
  5. 5Captain — type the captain's name, or leave as SELF if you were the captain
  6. 6Holder Operating Capacity (HOC) — use the dropdown:
    P1 = Captain  ·  P2 = First Officer  ·  P1 U/S = PIC Under Supervision
  7. 7Pilot Flying (PF) — tick ☑ if you were the handling pilot for that sector
  8. 8DEP / ARR — ICAO codes, e.g. WMKK / WSSS
  9. 9STD / STA — off-block and on-block in UTC format HH:MM (e.g. 06:30)
  10. 10Day / Night auto-fills — nothing to do. Calculated the moment you enter block times
Data saves automatically as you type. There is no save button.

Your logbook is organised by month. Jump to any month and year using the Period Selector, or use the shortcut in the Flight Summary tab.
  1. 1Find Select Period in the top-right corner of the screen
  2. 2Click the month dropdown and select the month you want (January–December)
  3. 3Click the year dropdown to change the year — 2024 through 2030 are supported
  4. 4The logbook updates immediately showing that month's entries
  5. 5Shortcut: open the Flight Summary tab and click any month row — it jumps straight to that logbook page
Each month holds up to 30 rows. The currently selected month is highlighted with a cyan left border in the Flight Summary.

The Flight Summary tab shows your entire year at a glance — monthly sector counts and hour totals broken down by day/night and capacity type (P1, P1 U/S, P2).
  1. 1Click the Flight Summary tab in the middle of the tab bar
  2. 2Each row = one month. Empty months show dashes (—)
  3. 3Sectors column = total number of flight sectors logged that month
  4. 4Hour columns split by Day / Night and by capacity: P1, P1 U/S, P2
  5. 5The Annual Total row at the bottom sums the full year
  6. 6Click any month row to jump directly to that month's logbook page
Use the annual total to track where you stand against yearly regulatory limits or personal hour targets.

The Limits & Recency tab automatically checks your flying against Malaysian aviation (CAD 1901) regulatory limits. Everything is calculated for you — just read the gauges.
  1. 1Click the Limits & Recency tab — the one with the green dot ●
  2. 2Read the status banner at the top: green ✔ = fully compliant, amber/red = approaching or exceeded a limit
  3. 3Flight Time Limits — hours flown in the last 28 consecutive days (cap: 100 hrs) and last 12 months (cap: 900 hrs)
  4. 4Cumulative Duty Hours — duty time in 7, 14, and 28 consecutive days vs regulatory maximums
  5. 5Takeoff & Landing Recency — select your aircraft type to check if you've completed 3 T&Ls in the last 90 days
  6. 6Autoland Recency — checks for 3 autoland approaches within the last 6 months (all aircraft types)
Green bar = safe. As hours accumulate the bar fills. Amber = approaching limit. Red = at or over the cap.

Settings lets you configure your profile, logbook preferences, and day/night calculation method. Access it any time via the ⚙ gear icon in the toolbar.
  1. 1Click the ⚙ gear icon in the top-right toolbar to open Settings
  2. 2Profile tab — enter your full name, date of birth, staff/employee ID, licence number, licence type, and airline
  3. 3Preferences tab — set your preferred date display format and rows per page (default: 30)
  4. 4Day/Night method — Fixed Boundary (simple: 11:30–23:30 UTC = night) or Dynamic CAD-6 (actual sunrise/sunset for your departure airport — more accurate)
  5. 5Carry Forward Hours — entering hours from a previous logbook ensures cumulative totals are correct from day one
  6. 6Click Save Changes — profile updates apply to the logbook immediately
Your email is linked to your Google account and cannot be changed here. The Miscellaneous tab contains the app changelog and version history.
